Clinical Guidelines & Protocols Hub

Quick-reference resources for evidence-based clinical practice.

Cardiovascular

Immediate Actions:
  • Assess ABCs and obtain vital signs.
  • Administer Aspirin 162-325 mg chewed.
  • Perform 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of presentation.
  • Establish IV access and obtain blood work (Cardiac biomarkers, CBC, CMP, Coags).
  • Administer Oxygen if Saturation < 90%.
Initial Assessment:
  • Determine Last Known Well (LKW) time.
  • Perform NIHSS (NIH Stroke Scale) assessment.
  • Stat non-contrast CT head to rule out hemorrhage.
  • Check point-of-care blood glucose.
  • Evaluate for tPA eligibility if within 4.5 hour window.

Primary Care Protocols

Diagnosis & Thresholds:
  • Normal: <120/<80 mmHg
  • Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
  • Stage 1 HTN: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
  • Stage 2 HTN: ≥140/≥90 mmHg
First-line therapies: Thiazide diuretics, CCBs, and ACE inhibitors or ARBs.

Emergency Response

First Line:

  • Repeat every 5-15 mins if no improvement.
Adjuncts: H1/H2 blockers, Corticosteroids, Albuterol for bronchospasm.

Clinical Guidelines & Management: Celiac Disease in Children

Executive Summary

An interactive clinical guide detailing the pathophysiology, diagnostic pathway (serology and biopsy), and long-term nursing follow-up for children with celiac disease.

Key Takeaways

  • Strict lifelong gluten-free diet (<20 ppm) is mandatory to prevent systemic damage.
  • Diagnosis requires serological testing (IgA TTG) and histological confirmation (biopsy).
  • Routine follow-up demands precise anthropometric monitoring, serology, and nutritional support.

Hypertension: Diagnosis, Evaluation, and Treatment

Executive Summary

An evidence-based medicine (EBM) clinical review integrating AHA/ACC guidelines with Israeli society guidelines, focusing on target populations.

Key Takeaways

  • First-line treatment is based on drug combinations (ACEi/ARB + CCB/Diuretics).
  • Strict BP targets (<130/80) for high-risk patients, versus more moderate targets in the elderly (140-150/90).
  • Out-of-office monitoring (HBPM/ABPM) is a critical component in accurate diagnosis.

Medical Clearance for Return to Educational Institutions

Executive Summary

Integration of Ministry of Health and IMA guidelines defining clinical criteria for children's return to educational settings following infectious illnesses.

Key Takeaways

  • Fever (≥38°C) requires exclusion until 24 hours pass without fever and without antipyretics.
  • Gastrointestinal infections require 24-48 hours symptom-free prior to return, depending on the pathogen.
  • Streptococcal tonsillitis requires 24 hours without fever and a minimum of 12 hours of antibiotic therapy.

Philosophy of Care: Medicine at Eye Level

My clinical worldview as a Family Medicine Nurse Practitioner relies on integrating medical professionalism with patient empowerment, guided by three core principles:

  • "If I am not for myself, who will be for me?" – Empowerment and Personal Responsibility: Healing begins with the patient. My role is to provide the knowledge, tools, and confidence necessary for patients to take control of their health, properly manage chronic illnesses, and make informed choices.
  • "And being only for myself, what am I?" – Clinical Alliance and Partnership: The patient never walks alone. I believe in medicine based on trust, listening, and continuous personal connection. In the clinic, I serve as a professional anchor and guide through moments of crisis and routine.
  • "And if not now, when?" – Preventive and Proactive Medicine: The best time to treat a disease is before it worsens or breaks out. My approach places supreme emphasis on preventive medicine, early detection, and rapid clinical intervention.
